Field Guide
01 Image Capture

The single biggest factor in a fast, clean run is flying high enough. Coverage and ground resolution trade off against each other — doubling altitude roughly quarters the photo count.

For most incident overlays, 60–100 m AGL is a good working band. Drop to 30–50 m only when fine detail genuinely matters. Fly higher when you can.

Overlap matters more than total image count. Aim for at least 75% frontal and 65% side overlap. More is better. Most mission-planning apps will calculate this for you; trust their planner over a manual count.

Flight pattern: Fly a lawnmower grid over the target area. For high-detail jobs where roofline and structure accuracy matters, fly the grid twice with the second pass rotated 90°. Maintain consistent altitude and let the mission planner set overlap automatically. Do not mix altitudes within a single job.

GPS lock must be solid before takeoff. Take a single image while still on the ground to estabish a baseline.

Limit: 100 photos per job.
02 Upload & Process

Enter an incident name or number in Section 1 — this becomes the output filename and how the overlay appears in CloudTAK. Use whatever your team will recognize in the field (e.g. Warehouse Fire, MM-12 South Fork) The name chosen will automatically have the date and time it was generated appended to the end of the file name.

Click the photo picker and select your GPS-tagged JPEG photos. The picker rejects non-JPEG files and photos missing GPS EXIF before processing starts.

Click Process. The upload bar runs first, then the job moves to Standby while the processing node builds the orthophoto. Typical run time is 3–5 minutes for a 30–70 photo job.

The page can be left open in the background — status updates on its own. If something goes wrong, the most common cause is a single photo with no GPS data; remove it and re-run.

03 Import to CloudTAK

When a job completes, two download buttons appear in Section 3: MBTILES and GEOTIFF RGB. Pick whichever your destination needs. GEOTIFF works for most apps. In CloudTAK open the Overlay Tool > "+" > "Your Files". "Your Files" is where the custom overlay files would appear. Use the "Upload Icon" to add the GEOTIFF

72-hour purge. Both files are automatically deleted from the server 72 hours after the job completes. Download what you need before then. Overlays made with sUAS not equipped with an RTK sensor may be up to +/- 10 meters in accuracy but are typically half that.
